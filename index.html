Rock Stars

Gerry Garcia

(August 1, 1942 - August 9, 1995) was an American musician known for being the principal songwriter, lead guitarist, and a vocalist with the rock band Grateful Dead, which he co-founded and which came to prominence during the counterculture of the 1960s.

Tina Turner

(November 26, 1939 - May 24, 2023) was a singer, songwriter, and actress. Known as the "Queen of Rock 'n' Roll", she rose to prominence as the lead singer of the husband-wife duo Ike & Tina Turner before launching a successful career as a solo performer.

Frank Zappa

(December 21, 1940 - December 4, 1993) was an American musician, composer, and bandleader. His work is characterized by nonconformity, free-form improvisation, sound experimentation, musical virtuosity, and satire of American culture.

Dolores O'Riordan

(September 6, 1971 - January 15, 2018) was an Irish singer, musician, and songwriter. She was the lead vocalist and lyricist of the alternative rock band The Cranberries.

Gwen Stefani

(October 3, 1969) is an American singer and songwriter. She is a co-founder, lead vocalist, and the primary songwriter of the band No Doubt, whose singles include "Just a Girl", "Spiderwebs", and "Don't Speak", from their 1995 breakthrough studio album Tragic Kingdom.

Elvis Presley

(January 8, 1935 - August 16, 1977), commonly referred to by his first name, was an American singer and actor. Known as the "King of Rock and Roll", he is regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century.
